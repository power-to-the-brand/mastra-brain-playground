# Design Context

### Users
**Developers & Engineers** testing and debugging agent logic, supervisor workflows, and AI output. They need a workspace that is highly functional but doesn't feel clinical or exhausting for long sessions.

### Brand Personality
**Warm & Cozy Lab**. The interface should feel like a comfortable, well-lit study or workshop. It balances the precision of a laboratory with the warmth of a cozy environment.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Refined Industrial / Editorial**. Think high-quality paper, subtle textures, soft shadows, and warm neutrals. It uses sharp typography (serif headings, refined mono for data) and purposeful spacing to create a sense of organized comfort.
- **Theme**: Full multi-theme support (Light and Dark) with oklch-based color tokens.
- **Key Visuals**: Warm stone/amber tones in light mode, deep slate/amber in dark mode.

### Design Principles
1. **Comfortable Precision**: Use generous white space and soft tones to reduce eye strain, but maintain sharp alignment and clear hierarchy for complex data.
2. **Materiality & Depth**: Use subtle shadows and layered surfaces (like paper on a desk) to create a sense of focus without overusing glassmorphism.
3. **Data-First Warmth**: Present code and logs in refined monospace fonts with syntax highlighting that matches the warm palette.
4. **Purposeful Motion**: Use staggered, smooth transitions for page loads and state changes to make the interface feel alive and responsive.
